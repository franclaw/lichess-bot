VARIANT 3: Describe 3 simple chess tactical or positional scenarios where the best move is absolutely obvious even to a beginner.
----------------------------------------
Here are three simple chess tactical or positional scenarios where the optimal move is very clear to a beginner:

1. **Check to Lighten the King**  
   If your king is facing a check (meaning the enemy knight or pawn directly threatens your king), the most logical move is usually to move the king to a safer square. For example, if you have a weakened king and a better square, simply check the light color. The beginner will immediately see that the king’s safety is at stake.

2. **Rook to Closest Square**  
   When your rook is adjacent to or even on the board, the best move is to push it toward the nearest square. For instance, if your rook is stuck in the corner, move it to a position that controls more territory or prepares a better attack opportunity. Beginners often catch this immediately since rook placement is straightforward.

3. **Pawn on e4 or e5 vs. Weak Pawn to Front**  
   After castling and securing your position, if your pawn on the same file is being pressured by the opponent’s pawn on the same file, moving the pawn forward (e.g., pushing it to e4, e5, etc.) is clear and safe. Beginners often recognize the importance of protecting their pawns and supporting front pressure, which is easy to spot.

These scenarios emphasize basic principles—king safety, piece development, and pawn structure—making them intuitive for a beginner.
