VARIANT 2: Can you provide 3 examples of chess positions where there is only one logical or winning move? Explain why.
----------------------------------------
Here are three examples of chess positions where only one logical or winning move exists, along with explanations:

---

### **Example 1: "Cornered Checkmate"**  
**Position**:  
- White king on d1.  
- Black king on e8.  
- White queen on g4, rook on h8, and other pieces blocked.  

**Reason**:  
The white king is exposed to check without any adjacent pieces to block its escape or prevent a counterforce. The black king cannot protect its position adequately, and any non-checkmaking move (e.g., moving a piece to disrupt) would expose the white king to further pressure. Thus, the **only logical move** is to either:  
- Move the white king (if allowed, but restricted by other constraints), risking material loss, **or**  
- Force the white king into checkmate if the queen or rook is delivered. Either way, advancing the piece creates a forcing move toward victory, while delaying risks stalemate or loss.  

---

### **Example 2: "Central Puzzled King"**  
**Position**:  
- Black king on f7.  
- White king on g5, surrounded by pawns on a7 and a8.  

**Reason**:  
The black king’s central position gives white kings tight control of the center. Any move by white must either:  
- Protect the white king by advancing or capturing, which would force the black king further into the center and likely expose itself to checkmate within a quick sequence.  
- If the white king attempts to distance itself, it leaves itself exposed, leaving the game in a tinderbox state. The minimal response—blocking the immediate threat—is the only path back to safety, making it the **only winning move**.  

---

### **Example 3: "Forced Checkmate Move"**  
**Position**:  
- White king on e8.  
- Black king on h6.  
- The remaining pieces on the board block escape routes.  

**Reason**:  
The black king has *no legal options* aside from one move—the knight could move to g7, but this only allows the black king to retreat toward h5, deepening check. Alternatively, moving other pieces into check positions would leave the white king vulnerable. The only viable choice is to:  
1. Forcing the black king into central opposition.  
2. **Exposing white king directly to checkmate**, requiring immediate action. Any delay risks a forced resignation. Hence, the single logical move is decisive.  

---

### Summary:  
In all cases, the position creates a **self-perpetrating threat** where any deviation leads to loss, and the sole response is to capitalize on the forced vulnerability. The other moves either result in material loss, stalemate, or prevent resolution—making the single path both necessary and optimal.

VARIANT 3: Describe 3 simple chess tactical or positional scenarios where the best move is absolutely obvious even to a beginner.
----------------------------------------
Here are three simple scenarios where the optimal move is immediately clear to beginners:

---

### 1. **The Blocked Rook**  
A rook is trapped behind an advancing pawn, preventing it from reaching its goal. The obvious move is to **push the pawn forward** to clear the path or **intercept the attacking piece**, forcing the opponent into a weak position. Beginners often fail to recognize the rook’s vulnerability here without deeper strategy.

---

### 2. **Passed Pawn Capture**  
A pawn is passed without opposing activity, creating an immediate opportunity. The clear choice is to **capture it decisively** on the back rank, halting its advance and starving the opponent of its control. New players often overlook early-entered pawns as expendable.

---

### 3. **Double Pawn Restriction**  
Two pawns advance to block a central square, splitting the opponent’s vision. The simplest fix is to **mobile a pawn** to create a double-pawn chain, limiting their mobility. Beginners often hesitate to disrupt such basic structure, allowing their opponent to dominate the board.

---

In each case, the "best move" stems from **immediate positional control** or **immediate consequence**, making it effortless for novices to grasp without prior complexity.
